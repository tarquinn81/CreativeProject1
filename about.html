About Bucky Kentucky

  I grew up in the most country best state Tennessee. I've never been a big city kind of boy. I love country music, my hometown, and my country. I've never traveled out of state yet, because I don't need to! I live in the best state in the country and my family has been here for generations. We aren't planning on betraying our family name and leaving Tennessee any time soon. No the Kentucky's are here to stay.

  I remember when I first picked up a guitar. I was at my friend's Murrey Missouri's house. I couldn't have been older than 6 years old. His dad had an old guitar and as soon as I picked up the Missouri's old Tennessee instrument, I knew I never wanted to put it down. I played for hours on that thing learning and writing all sorts of songs. Most of them about my great state.

  I have always looked up to my mentor my amazing friend and teacher Bhillip Rhode Island. He's taught me everything I know about being true to my name and who I am. He's an amazing musician and he's kept me out of the trouble the music industry can get you into. I am proud of the friends he's introduced me to like, Aubriana Indiana, Paulo New Mexico and Kyle Ohio. The country music scene is full of great people you just need to find them. I will always look up to these people. That's the great thing about music you meet people just like you.

  My previous band the Kentucky 5 broke up because they never really were true to what makes a Kentucky man, a Kentucky man (I would know). They kept talking about the state and how important it is. A real Kentucky man cares about their home in Tennessee. It's never been about the state. They wanted to get a sponsorship from KFC, but I'm more of a fan of Tennessee chicken. It wasn't going to work from the beginning. That's why I'm going off on my own. I'm here to really hold true to my Kentucky family values (KFV).

Jacob Bastian & Quinn Karpowitz ©2022
